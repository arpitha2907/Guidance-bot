# First Aid for Minor Burns (General Guidance)

Minor burns are small, surface burns that cause redness and mild pain but do not break deeply into the skin. They commonly happen from hot liquids, steam, or brief contact with a hot surface.

Cool the burn as soon as possible by holding it under cool (not ice-cold) running water for about twenty minutes. Remove any rings or tight items near the area before it swells. After cooling, cover the burn loosely with a clean, non-fluffy dressing or cling film to protect it. Do not apply butter, toothpaste, or ice, as these can cause further damage.

Over-the-counter pain relief can help with discomfort. Keep the area clean and avoid bursting any blisters that form.

Seek medical care for burns that are larger than the affected person's hand, are deep or charred or white, involve the face, hands, feet, or genitals, or result from chemicals or electricity. Burns in young children or older adults should be assessed by a professional.

This information is general guidance only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice.
