# Recognising and Managing Dehydration (General Guidance)

Dehydration happens when the body loses more fluid than it takes in. It can result from hot weather, exercise, fever, vomiting, or diarrhoea. Early signs include thirst, a dry mouth, dark yellow urine, passing urine less often, tiredness, and dizziness.

For mild dehydration, sip water steadily rather than drinking large amounts at once. Oral rehydration solutions are helpful when fluid loss is due to vomiting or diarrhoea, as they replace lost salts as well as water. Avoid sugary or caffeinated drinks as the main source of rehydration.

Prevent dehydration by drinking regularly through the day, especially in heat or during exercise and illness. Watch young children and older adults closely, as they are more vulnerable.

Seek medical care if there are signs of severe dehydration such as extreme thirst, very little or no urination, sunken eyes, rapid heartbeat or breathing, confusion, or fainting. Severe dehydration is a medical emergency.

This information is general guidance only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice.
