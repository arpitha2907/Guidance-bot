# Managing a Common Headache (General Guidance)

Most headaches are not serious and ease with simple self-care. Common types include tension headaches, which often feel like a tight band around the head, and headaches brought on by dehydration, hunger, eye strain, lack of sleep, or stress.

For relief, rest in a quiet, dimly lit room and drink water, since mild dehydration is a frequent trigger. A cool cloth on the forehead or gentle massage of the neck and temples can help. Try to keep regular meals and sleep, and take breaks from screens.

Over-the-counter pain relievers can help occasional headaches, but follow the dosage on the packaging and avoid taking them more than a couple of days a week, as frequent use can lead to rebound headaches.

Seek medical care promptly if a headache is sudden and severe ("the worst headache of your life"), follows a head injury, or comes with fever and a stiff neck, confusion, weakness or numbness, trouble speaking, vision loss, or a seizure. Also see a doctor if headaches are becoming more frequent or severe over time.

This information is general guidance only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice.
