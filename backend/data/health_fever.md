# Managing a Mild Fever at Home (General Guidance)

A fever is the body's natural response to infection and is usually not dangerous on its own. For a mild fever, rest and staying hydrated are the most important steps. Drink water, clear soups, or oral rehydration fluids regularly.

Dress in light clothing and keep the room at a comfortable temperature. Avoid bundling up in heavy blankets, as this can trap heat.

Over-the-counter fever reducers may help with comfort, but always follow the dosage instructions on the packaging and do not combine multiple products without checking.

Seek medical care promptly if the fever is very high, lasts more than three days, or comes with a stiff neck, difficulty breathing, persistent vomiting, confusion, or a rash that does not fade when pressed. For infants and young children, contact a doctor early rather than waiting.

This information is general guidance only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice.
