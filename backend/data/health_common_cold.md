# Coping with a Common Cold (General Guidance)

A cold is a viral infection of the nose and throat. Typical symptoms include a runny or blocked nose, sore throat, sneezing, cough, and mild tiredness. Most colds clear up on their own within seven to ten days.

Rest and fluids are the mainstays of recovery. Drink water, warm soups, or warm drinks to stay hydrated and soothe the throat. Saltwater gargles can ease a sore throat, and a humidifier or steam may relieve congestion. Honey can help calm a cough in adults and children over one year of age.

Antibiotics do not work against colds because colds are caused by viruses, not bacteria. Over-the-counter remedies may ease symptoms but do not cure the cold; follow the dosage instructions.

See a doctor if symptoms last beyond about ten days or worsen, if there is a high or persistent fever, difficulty breathing, chest pain, or if symptoms are severe in an infant, older adult, or someone with a weakened immune system.

This information is general guidance only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice.
