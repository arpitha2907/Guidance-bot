# Workplace Disputes: General Guidance

For concerns like unpaid wages, unclear termination, or unfair treatment at work, start by reviewing your employment contract or offer letter and any employee handbook, since these usually set out the agreed terms and internal complaint process.

Keep a written record of relevant events, including dates, what was said or done, and any related emails or messages. This record can be important if the issue needs to be escalated later.

Most workplaces have an internal process for raising grievances -- HR, a manager, or (for harassment complaints) an internal committee required under many countries' workplace harassment laws. Raising the issue internally first, in writing, is often the recommended first step and may resolve things faster than external action.

If the matter is not resolved internally, external options such as a labour department, employment tribunal, or ombudsman may be available depending on your location and the nature of the dispute. Serious matters like harassment, discrimination, or unsafe working conditions may warrant seeking legal advice sooner rather than later.

This is general information only, not legal advice specific to your employment situation. Employment law varies by country and region -- for advice on your specific case, consult a licensed employment lawyer.
