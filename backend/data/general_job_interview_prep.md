# Preparing for a Job Interview (General Guidance)

Start by researching the company and the role: understand what the organization does, recent news about it, and how the job description maps to your own experience. Prepare a few concrete examples from your past work or projects that demonstrate relevant skills.

A widely used way to structure answers to "tell me about a time when..." questions is the STAR method: describe the Situation, the Task you needed to accomplish, the Action you took, and the Result. This keeps answers concrete and easy to follow.

Prepare two or three thoughtful questions to ask the interviewer -- about the team, expectations for the role, or what success looks like in the first few months. This shows genuine interest and helps you evaluate the role too.

Practical logistics matter: confirm the format (in-person, phone, or video call), test your tech setup in advance for video interviews, and plan to arrive or log in a few minutes early.

This is general guidance only. Specific interview expectations vary a lot by company, industry, and role -- tailor your preparation accordingly.
