# Filing a Consumer Complaint (General Guidance)

If a product is defective or a service was not delivered as promised, start by raising the issue directly with the seller or service provider in writing (email or a written message), clearly describing the problem and what resolution you want (repair, replacement, or refund). Keep copies of receipts, invoices, advertisements, and any communication -- these records matter if the dispute continues.

Give the business a reasonable, clearly stated window to respond before escalating. Many companies have a formal grievance or customer-support escalation process; check their website or app for this before going further.

If the issue is not resolved directly, consumer protection mechanisms exist in most places to help, such as a national consumer helpline, an online consumer grievance portal, or a local consumer disputes forum where complaints can be filed, often for a modest fee for lower-value claims. Requirements and processes vary by location and by the value of the claim.

For high-value disputes, complex contracts, or if a business is unresponsive to formal channels, it is worth consulting a lawyer who can advise on the specific rules that apply in your area.

This is general information only, not legal advice specific to your situation or jurisdiction. For advice on your particular case, consult a licensed legal professional or your local consumer protection office.
